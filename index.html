What Problem Needs to Be Solved?

Access to sustainable and cost-effective cooking solutions is limited in areas where conventional energy sources are scarce or expensive. Additionally, traditional cooking methods often rely on fuels like wood or gas, contributing to environmental degradation and air pollution. A solar oven addresses these challenges by harnessing sunlight as a clean, renewable energy source for cooking.

Design Requirements (Part 1)

  • Efficient Heat Retention: Trap and maintain heat effectively to cook food.
  • Light Reflection: Reflective surfaces must direct sunlight into the box.
  • Insulation: Proper insulation is needed to minimize heat loss.
  • Compact and Lightweight: The design must be portable and easy to set up.

Design Requirements (Part 2)

  • Safety: Materials used should be safe to handle.
  • Cost-Effectiveness: Use inexpensive materials like pizza boxes and foil.
  • Simplicity: Construction should be simple for beginners.
  • Durability: Oven should withstand outdoor conditions.

Constraints (Part 1)

  • Budget: Use affordable, household materials.
  • Time: Construction should take less than an hour.
  • Materials: Use lightweight, low-cost, and heat-safe items.
  • Weather Dependence: Rely entirely on sunlight.

Constraints (Part 2)

  • Size: Oven must balance size for food and heat retention.
  • Heat Limitation: Moderate temperatures for light cooking only.
  • User Skill Level: Simple enough for children with adult supervision.

Brainstorm: Pizza Box Solar Oven

Description: A pizza box with aluminum foil lining and black construction paper absorbs heat, with plastic wrap trapping it inside.
