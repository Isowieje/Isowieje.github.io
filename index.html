What Problem Needs to Be Solved?

Access to sustainable and cost-effective cooking solutions is limited in areas where conventional energy sources are scarce or expensive. Additionally, traditional cooking methods often rely on fuels like wood or gas, contributing to environmental degradation and air pollution.

A solar oven addresses these challenges by harnessing sunlight as a clean, renewable energy source for cooking.

Design Requirements (Part 1)

  • Efficient Heat Retention: The oven must trap and maintain heat effectively to cook food using only sunlight.
  • Light Reflection: Reflective surfaces must direct sunlight into the box to maximize heat.
  • Insulation: Proper insulation is needed to minimize heat loss and retain warmth inside the box.
  • Compact and Lightweight: The design must be portable and easy to set up.

Design Requirements (Part 2)

  • Safety: The materials used should be safe to handle and pose no risk when exposed to heat.
  • Cost-Effectiveness: Use inexpensive, readily available materials like pizza boxes, foil, and plastic wrap.
  • Simplicity: The construction process must be simple enough for beginners or children to follow.
  • Durability: The oven should withstand outdoor conditions during use.

Constraints (Part 1)

  • Budget: Materials must be affordable and sourced easily from household items or stores.
  • Time: Construction should take under an hour; cooking time for marshmallows is 30–40 minutes after preheating.
  • Materials: Only lightweight, low-cost materials like cardboard, aluminum foil, plastic wrap, and duct tape can be used.
  • Weather Dependence: Relies entirely on sunlight, making it ineffective in cloudy or rainy weather.

Constraints (Part 2)

  • Size: The oven must be large enough to accommodate food items but small enough to maintain heat efficiently.
  • Heat Limitation: The oven will only reach moderate temperatures, restricting it to cooking lightweight items.
  • User Skill Level: The design must be simple enough for young participants with adult supervision.

Brainstorm

Pizza Box Solar Oven

A pizza box with aluminum foil lining, black construction paper on the base for heat absorption, and a plastic wrap cover to trap heat.

Strengths: Simple to build and uses common materials.

Ideal Use: For light cooking, such as melting chocolate.

Brainstorm

Shoebox Solar Oven

A shoebox with black construction paper lining, aluminum foil reflectors, and plastic wrap sealing the top opening to trap heat.

Strengths: Compact and easy to adjust reflectors.

Ideal Use: Great for quick experiments with smaller food items.

Discussion: Why I Chose This Design

I chose the pizza box solar oven design over the shoebox design because I didn’t have a shoebox available, and pizza boxes are often easy to get for free if you simply ask.

Equipment/Materials

  • Utility knife
  • Scissors
  • Masking tape
  • Black duct tape
  • Aluminum foil tape
  • School glue
  • Plastic wrap
  • Reflective insulation
  • Aluminum foil
  • Chocolate
  • Paper plate
  • Black construction paper

Procedure (Part 1)

1. Prepare the Pizza Box: Draw lines to create a flap and cut.

2. Add Aluminum Foil for Insulation: Attach foil to the bottom of the pizza box.

3. Add a Heat-Absorbing Surface: Glue black construction paper on top of the foil.

4. Prepare the Reflective Flap: Attach reflective insulation to the flap.

Procedure (Part 2)

5. Seal the Box with Plastic Wrap: Tape plastic wrap over the hole to trap heat.

6. Adjust the Flap: Use a straw or stick to hold the flap open at an angle to reflect sunlight.

7. Cook the Chocolate: Place chocolate on a paper plate and seal the box.

In Files

Testing First Prototype

A piece of chocolate was placed on a paper plate and sealed inside the solar oven with plastic wrap to trap heat. The black construction paper absorbed sunlight, and the plastic wrap helped retain the heat, melting the chocolate despite not preheating the oven.

Redesign

I improved my design by adding more aluminum foil than necessary. I also added a straw to hold up the flap so it doesn't fly away.

Conclusion

Pizza box solar ovens are an excellent example of how simple materials can be used to harness solar energy effectively.

They are inexpensive, eco-friendly, and easy to build, making them accessible for educational purposes and promoting awareness of renewable energy.
