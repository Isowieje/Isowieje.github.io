What Problem Needs to Be Solved?

Access to sustainable and cost-effective cooking solutions is limited in areas where conventional energy sources are scarce or expensive. Traditional cooking methods often rely on fuels like wood or gas, contributing to environmental degradation and air pollution.

A solar oven addresses these challenges by harnessing sunlight as a clean, renewable energy source for cooking.

Design Requirements (Part 1)

- Efficient Heat Retention: Trap and maintain heat effectively to cook food using only sunlight.
- Light Reflection: Reflective surfaces must direct sunlight into the box to maximize heat.
- Insulation: Minimize heat loss and retain warmth inside the box.
- Compact and Lightweight: Ensure portability and ease of setup.

Design Requirements (Part 2)

- Safety: Use materials safe to handle and heat-resistant.
- Cost-Effectiveness: Use inexpensive, readily available materials like pizza boxes, foil, and plastic wrap.
- Simplicity: Keep the construction process simple enough for beginners or children.
- Durability: Ensure the oven can withstand outdoor conditions like wind and sunlight.

Constraints (Part 1)

- Budget: Use affordable materials sourced from household items or stores.
- Time: Construction should take under an hour, with a cooking time of 30–40 minutes.
- Materials: Use lightweight, low-cost materials that withstand heat without melting or releasing harmful substances.
- Weather Dependence: Relies entirely on sunlight, making it ineffective in cloudy or rainy weather.

Constraints (Part 2)

- Size: Must be large enough for food but small enough to maintain heat efficiently.
- Heat Limitation: Limited to moderate temperatures, suitable for lightweight items like chocolate.
- User Skill Level: Simple enough for children working with adult supervision.

Brainstorm

Pizza Box Solar Oven:
- Description: A pizza box with aluminum foil lining, black construction paper for heat absorption, and plastic wrap cover to trap heat. Reflective insulation focuses sunlight into the box.
- Strengths: Simple to build using common materials.
- Ideal Use: Light cooking like melting chocolate.

Discussion

I chose the pizza box solar oven design over the shoebox design because I didn’t have a shoebox available, and pizza boxes are often easy to get for free by simply asking.

Equipment/Materials

- Utility knife, scissors, masking tape, black duct tape
- Aluminum foil tape, school glue, plastic wrap
- Reflective insulation, aluminum foil, chocolate, paper plate
- Black construction paper

Procedure (Part 1)

1. Prepare the pizza box by cutting and folding a flap.
2. Add aluminum foil for insulation.
3. Add black construction paper for heat absorption.
4. Prepare a reflective flap with insulation.

Procedure (Part 2)

5. Seal the box with plastic wrap.
6. Adjust the flap using a straw or stick to reflect sunlight.
7. Place chocolate inside and seal the box.
8. Cook the chocolate and enjoy!

Testing First Prototype

Chocolate was placed on a plate and sealed in the solar oven. The black construction paper absorbed sunlight, and plastic wrap retained heat, allowing the chocolate to melt even without preheating.

Redesign

I improved the design by adding more aluminum foil for better insulation. I also added a straw to hold the flap open so it wouldn’t fly away.

Conclusion

Pizza box solar ovens demonstrate how simple materials can harness solar energy effectively. They are inexpensive, eco-friendly, and accessible for educational purposes, promoting awareness of renewable energy.
