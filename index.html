What problem needs to be solved?

Access to sustainable and cost-effective cooking solutions is limited in areas where conventional energy sources are scarce or expensive.

Additionally, traditional cooking methods often rely on fuels like wood or gas, contributing to environmental degradation and air pollution.

A solar oven addresses these challenges by harnessing sunlight as a clean, renewable energy source for cooking.

Design Requirements pt 1

  • Efficient Heat Retention: The oven must trap and maintain heat effectively to cook food, such as chocolate, using only sunlight.
  • Light Reflection: Reflective surfaces must direct sunlight into the box to maximize heat.
  • Insulation: Proper insulation is needed to minimize heat loss and retain warmth inside the box.
  • Compact and Lightweight: The design must be portable and easy to set up.

Design Requirements pt 2

  • Safety: The materials used should be safe to handle and pose no risk when exposed to heat.
  • Cost-Effectiveness: Use inexpensive, readily available materials like pizza boxes, foil, and plastic wrap.
  • Simplicity: The construction process must be simple enough for beginners or children to follow.
  • Durability: The oven should withstand outdoor conditions (e.g., wind or sunlight exposure) during use.

Constraints pt 1

  • Budget: The materials must be affordable and sourced easily from household items or stores.
  • Time: Construction should take under an hour; cooking time for marshmallows is 30–40 minutes after preheating.
  • Materials: Lightweight, low-cost materials like cardboard, aluminum foil, plastic wrap, and duct tape must withstand heat without melting or releasing harmful substances.
  • Weather Dependence: Solar oven relies entirely on sunlight, making it ineffective in cloudy or rainy weather.

Constraints pt 2

  • Size: The oven must be large enough to accommodate food items like chocolate but small enough to maintain heat efficiently.
  • Heat Limitation: Only moderate temperatures, restricting cooking to lightweight items like chocolate.
  • User Skill Level: Simple enough for young participants (e.g., children working with adult supervision).

Brainstorm

Pizza Box Solar Oven: A pizza box with aluminum foil lining on the bottom and lid flap, black construction paper on the base, and plastic wrap cover to trap heat.

Strengths: Simple to build and uses common materials.

Ideal Use: Light cooking, such as melting chocolate.

Discussion

I chose the pizza box solar oven design over the shoebox design because I didn’t have a shoebox available, and pizza boxes are often easy to get for free if you simply ask.
